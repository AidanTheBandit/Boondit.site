## <My funny history with Rabbit />

I saw the launch at CES and wanted one so badly. For Christmas my grandmother got me an R1 and I loved it. The problem was mine had "the rattle". After fighting with support for a month I joined the Discord hoping for better support. I ended up staying because the community was so fun and I still loved my R1. I wasnt mad that it was defective, I just wanted to enjoy it again.

After complaining to Simon a LOT I finally got my replacement. My first major R1 project was the shell swap, a custom modeled shell to replace the OEM one (and you can 3D print it).

## <Creations happened />

When creations released as part of OS2 (little web based apps) I fell in love with how much more functionality it added. I wanted to push the limits. I first tested the waters with R1-Walky, one of the first creations. Then I tried more using sockets to build some multiplayer stuff.

My first big break was R1-Anywhere, a way to use R1 as an OpenAI compliant API. When I published it, I got a message from Jesse, the CEO of Rabbit. He wanted a meeting. I cant go into specifics, but Rabbit wasnt mad at all and were very impressed. That was the first time the idea of an internship was thrown out there.

A month later I demoed a new POC called "intern plugin" which allowed RabbitOS Intern to make creations that had a working and hosted backend. That got me a meeting with Jesse and the founding engineer. A while after that I joined the alpha testing group, something I never thought would happen.

## <The offer />

After a few months of very exciting testing I got an internship offer from Jesse which made my whole week. Im still so excited to be here. Words cant describe how badly Ive wanted to work at Rabbit and I cant wait to contribute to something great.
